ABOUT US

Nasijona

Nasijona was established in 2014 in Nazareth by the CEO Violette Khoury, as a registered non-profit organization with a twelve member Executive Board. At the moment, our organization includes 70 volunteers and 20 employees. The organization came in response to the suffering of the Arab-Palestinian society, which is losing its identity, as well as the memory, and the history., including the language and the heritage. Nasijona creates opportunities for the women of Nazareth to work together to resurrect and preserve local traditions through workshops and educational, artistic and cultural activities such as knitting, jewelry- making and Arabic Calligraphy. We aim to empower women economically, culturally and socially by giving them the opportunity to produce and sell unique hand-made crafts as merchandise. By sharing art and culture, we restore social harmony to local communities. In addition to the apprenticeship and workshops for youth, children and women, Nasijona also runs special training for people with special needs.

CEO: Violette Khoury


OUR ACTIVITIES

Hands-on Needlework Workshopsfor the Women of Nazareth.

Nasijona coordinates one-on-one training workshops for women, children and youth in various traditional artistic needlework such as tatting, crochet or shuttle.
In addition, women can learn knitting, Arabic calligraphy, embroidery, and jewelry making.

Social and Cultural Projects

Nasijona also holds social and cultural events such as artisan training workshops, seminars for writers and literary figures, as well as periodic cultural events in the city center.

Hosting Visitors and Tourists

We welcome visitors from outside of Nazareth and tourist groups at our cultural center. To accommodate our guests, wehost lectures, meeting&greet with the local artists, cultural sessions for traditional stories, and gallery displays of the unique artistic work produced by the hands of Nazareth's women.
